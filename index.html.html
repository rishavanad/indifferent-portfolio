MANIFEST / TF-002 / WEEK 2–3 Delivered

TrustFix

A two-sided home services marketplace — verified professionals, fixed upfront pricing, and a guided step-by-step booking flow.

Problem

Booking home services (plumbers, electricians, cleaners) is unpredictable — no verified credentials, no upfront pricing, and no reliable way to know who's actually showing up.

Process

Designed the MVP around verification, fixed pricing, ratings, and in-app payments, then converted the wireframes into a fully clickable HTML/JS prototype covering both the customer booking flow and the professional's job-acceptance flow.

Outcome

Shipped a working prototype, hosted live on Netlify, plus a slide deck walking through the design decisions.

What building (not just designing) taught me: while wiring up the prototype I hit a request-timeout edge case that never showed up in the static wireframes — a reminder that a design only reveals its real gaps once it's actually interactive.

MANIFEST / TH-001 / WEEK 1 Delivered

TrustHire

A product teardown of LinkedIn's job listings, and a concept for verified postings.

Problem

LinkedIn's job listings have no reliable verification layer — fake or stale postings erode trust in the platform's core job-search function.

Process

Broke down LinkedIn's existing job-posting flow to isolate exactly where trust breaks down, then proposed TrustHire — a verification layer for listings.

Outcome

A structured teardown and concept proposal — my first full product exercise in Product Matters 6.0.

MANIFEST / CC-003 / JTBD STUDY Delivered

Cart with Context

A Jobs-to-be-Done study on why people really use Amazon's shopping cart.

Problem

Philonet founder Rahul Mattihalli set a JTBD learning exercise during a Product Matters session: pick a product you use daily, find a real frustration with it, and validate the underlying "job" with actual people before building anything.

Process

Chose Amazon's shopping cart as the product and hypothesized that people repurpose it as a long-term decision space rather than a pure checkout step, then validated that hypothesis with a survey.

Outcome

Proposed an MVP concept, "Cart with Context," and packaged the findings into a slide deck.
